Findable Principle

Data and metadata should be easily discoverable by both humans and machines.

  • F1: (Meta)data are assigned a globally unique and persistent identifier.
  • F2: Data are described with rich metadata.
  • F3: Metadata clearly include the identifier of the data they describe.
  • F4: (Meta)data are registered or indexed in a search resource.

Accessible Principle

Data and metadata should be accessible through well-defined and open protocols.

  • A1: (Meta)data are retrievable by their identifier using a standardized communication protocol.
  • A1.1: The protocol is open, free, and universally implementable.
  • A1.2: The protocol allows an authentication and authorization procedure, when needed.
  • A2: Metadata are accessible even when the data are no longer available.

Interoperable Principle

Data should be integrable with other data and should interoperate with applications or workflows for analysis, storage, and processing.

  • I1: (Meta)data use a formal, accessible, shared, and applicable language for knowledge representation.
  • I2: (Meta)data use vocabularies that follow FAIR principles.
  • I3: (Meta)data include qualified references to other (meta)data.

Reusable Principle

Data should be reusable and prepared for future uses.

  • R1: (Meta)data are described with a plurality of precise and relevant attributes.
  • R1.1: (Meta)data are published with a clear and accessible data use license.
  • R1.2: (Meta)data are associated with provenance details.
  • R1.3: (Meta)data comply with relevant community standards.
